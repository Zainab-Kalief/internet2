Clients and Servers

Computers connected to the Web are called clients and servers.

  • Clients are the typical Web user's internet-connected devices (for example, your comuter connected to your wif-fi, or your phone connected to your mobile network) and Web-accessing software available on those devices (usually a web browser like Firefoc or Chrome).
  • Servers are computers that store webpages, sites or apps. When a clent device wants to acces a webpage, a copy of the webpage is downloaded from the serveer onto the client machine to be displayed in the user's web browser.
